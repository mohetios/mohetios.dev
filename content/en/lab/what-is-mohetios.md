Mohetios is a builder persona, a personal engineering workshop, and a name for the way I think through systems.

It is the public shape of a private habit: collecting ideas, testing small proofs, writing down tradeoffs, and slowly turning unclear thoughts into useful software.

The name begins with `Mohet`: personal, direct, and close to the feeling of “I am”.

It ends with `-ios`: technical, old-sounding, almost like the name of a tool, a system, or a small school of thought.

That combination matters.

Mohetios is personal, but it is not only about me. It gives a name to the space between private notes and finished products — the place where most real work actually happens.

Early ideas live there.  
Technical experiments live there.  
Architecture sketches live there.  
Unfinished drafts live there.  
Open-source tools live there.  
Mistakes, revisions, and small proofs live there too.

I needed a name for that space.

Not a company name.  
Not a startup mask.  
Not a polished portfolio pretending that every project arrived fully formed.

Mohetios is closer to a workshop.

A workshop is allowed to contain tools, notes, prototypes, fragments, strange ideas, and useful things that are still becoming what they should be. It does not hide the process. It gives the process a place to live.

That is the point of Mohetios.dev.

It is where I write about software, product systems, web architecture, cloud-native tools, AI-assisted workflows, open-source experiments, and the practical decisions behind building things that need to work.

Some pieces are polished case studies.  
Some are technical notes.  
Some are research fragments.  
Some are records of how an idea changed while I was building it.

The site is also a professional surface. It shows my work, my writing, my projects, and the systems I care about. But I do not want it to feel like a cold résumé page. I want it to feel like a living engineering notebook: calm, useful, readable, and honest.

## The Accidental Echo

There is also something in the sound of the name.

Some people hear Mohetios and think of mojito. Some may hear a faint echo of mojo. I like that accident.

The name does not come from those words, but the sound carries a useful tension: something personal, slightly strange, memorable, and quietly alive.

For me, Mohetios begins with `Mohet` and ends with `-ios`. But around it, there is also a small trace of craft, energy, and almost-magic — the kind that good work sometimes has.

Not fantasy magic.  
Not hype.  
Not mystery for its own sake.

The quiet magic of making things.

The moment when scattered notes become a system.  
When a vague product idea becomes an interface.  
When a prototype starts to behave like something useful.  
When the pieces finally fit together and the work begins to feel alive.

## The Working Pattern

Mohetios gives a stable name to a simple way of working:

Build small proofs.  
Write down the tradeoffs.  
Let ideas stay visible long enough to become useful.  
Turn vague thoughts into working systems.  
Keep the process open enough that others can learn from it.

That pattern is not only about software. It is about attention. It is about giving unfinished things enough structure to grow without pretending they are already complete.

Mohetios is where I keep that process visible.

## The Standard

Years ago, I used to write a sentence under the websites I designed:

Everything, in a wonderfully good way, must be.

The original Persian sentence is:

همه چیز به شکل شگفت‌انگیزی خوب باید باشد.

It is intentionally a little unusual. It does not sound like a normal slogan. It feels closer to a personal standard.

Not perfection.  
Not decoration.  
Not speed for its own sake.

A standard of care.

Care in how things are named.  
Care in how systems are shaped.  
Care in how interfaces behave.  
Care in how decisions are explained.  
Care in how unfinished work is allowed to become better.

Mohetios is where ideas unfold into systems.

And that old sentence still sits behind it.
