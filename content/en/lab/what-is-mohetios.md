Mohetios is a builder persona, a personal workshop, and a name for the way I think through systems.

The name begins with `Mohet`, a personal and colloquial form connected to the sense of “I am”. The suffix `-ios` gives it the feeling of an old technical name: half imagined, half inherited from the sound of Greek and Roman naming patterns.

That mix matters.

Mohetios is personal, but it is not only about me. It is a place for the kind of work that usually sits between private notes and finished products: early ideas, technical experiments, product decisions, architecture sketches, open-source tools, unfinished drafts, and systems that slowly become clearer through use.

I needed a name for that space.

Not a company name.
Not a startup mask.
Not a polished portfolio pretending that every project arrived fully formed.

Mohetios is closer to a workshop.

A workshop is allowed to contain tools, notes, prototypes, mistakes, revisions, strange ideas, and useful things that are still becoming what they should be. It does not hide the process. It gives the process a place to live.

That is the point of Mohetios.dev.

It is where I write about engineering, product systems, web architecture, cloud-native tools, AI-assisted workflows, open-source experiments, and the practical tradeoffs behind building software. Some pieces are polished case studies. Some are technical notes. Some are research fragments. Some are simply records of how an idea changed while I was building it.

The site is also a professional surface. It shows my work, my writing, my projects, and the systems I care about. But I do not want it to feel like a cold résumé page. I want it to feel like a living engineering notebook: calm, useful, readable, and honest.

Mohetios gives a stable name to a simple working pattern:

Build small proofs.
Write down the tradeoffs.
Let ideas stay visible long enough to become useful.
Turn vague thoughts into working systems.
Keep the process open enough that others can learn from it.

That is why the name matters. It gives shape to a way of working that is both technical and personal.

Mohetios is where ideas unfold into systems.

And behind all of it, there is still the old sentence I used to write under the websites I designed years ago:

Everything, in a wonderfully good way, must be.
