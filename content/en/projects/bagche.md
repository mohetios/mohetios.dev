Bagche is a small product workspace around commerce, publishing, and reusable web infrastructure. Its public shape is still narrow: a Bagche organization surface, the Mamoochi framework, and a set of ideas around lightweight commerce that should not be described too loudly before the product boundary is clear.

That is why this page stays in private review. The useful public story is not "a commerce platform." It is the slower work of finding the right shape for product systems that need a public face, a technical foundation, and room to become real without becoming heavy too early.

Repositories:

- [bagche/mamoochi](https://github.com/bagche/mamoochi)
- [bagche/Front](https://github.com/bagche/Front)

## Why It Exists

Bagche started from a familiar pressure: small product ideas often need more than a landing page, but less than a full platform.

They need a place to explain themselves.  
They need a content model.  
They need deployment that does not turn into operations work.  
They need enough structure that future features can attach cleanly.

For commerce and marketplace ideas, this pressure arrives early. The product surface may look simple, but underneath it there are questions about trust, listings, identity, publishing, updates, and how much coordination should happen before money or inventory enters the system.

## What It Explores

The Bagche thread is about small, composable product infrastructure:

- public-facing organization pages,
- Git-friendly content and publishing,
- Nuxt and Vue as the application layer,
- Cloudflare-friendly deployment,
- reusable framework work through Mamoochi,
- space for P2P commerce experiments without committing to a large architecture too soon.

The interesting part is the restraint. Bagche is useful as a named workspace because it can hold product ideas while the technical foundation is still being tested.

## How It Works

The public technical surface is intentionally simple. Mamoochi carries the framework side: Nuxt, Vue, Jamstack assumptions, and Cloudflare-compatible deployment. The Bagche front page gives the organization a visible shell.

Private or unfinished product ideas should stay private until they can be described without leaking details or pretending the system is more mature than it is.

## What I Learned

A product organization can be useful before it is a finished product.

It can collect naming, design direction, technical defaults, and small experiments in one place. But that only works if the boundary is honest. Bagche should not over-explain future commerce ideas before the workflow, trust model, and public responsibilities are ready.

## Current Status

Private review. The public repositories are visible, but the product story still needs editing before it should be treated as a complete case study.
