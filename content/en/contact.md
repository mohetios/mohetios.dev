The best way to reach me is with a short, specific message.

I read direct messages carefully, especially when they explain what exists today, what is unclear or blocked, and what kind of help would make the next step easier.

You do not need to write a long brief. A useful note is enough.

## Good Reasons To Reach Out

I am usually open to conversations around:

- product engineering and technical product work
    
- frontend, full-stack, and web platform architecture
    
- React, Next.js, Vue, Nuxt, TypeScript, GraphQL, and API-driven systems
    
- dashboards, admin panels, internal tools, and operational workflows
    
- Cloudflare-native, serverless, and edge-oriented software
    
- AI-assisted product and engineering workflows with real constraints
    
- technical writing, documentation, and engineering case studies
    
- open-source experiments and practical collaboration
    
- early product ideas that need clearer technical shape
    

Most good conversations start with a real problem, not a vague request.

## What To Include

If you are reaching out about a project, include:

- what the product or system does
    
- what stage it is in now
    
- what is blocked or unclear
    
- what kind of role or help you are looking for
    
- expected timeline
    
- budget, rate range, or engagement model if relevant
    
- links to the product, repository, design, document, or existing material
    

If you are reaching out about a role, include:

- company or client name
    
- role description
    
- contract type
    
- compensation or rate range
    
- timezone expectations
    
- remote policy
    
- hiring process
    

Clear context saves time for both sides.

## What Fits Best

The strongest fit is work where product thinking and engineering execution need to meet.

That can be a web platform, a dashboard, an internal system, a workflow, a technical migration, a product prototype, an AI-assisted feature, or a software system that needs to become simpler, clearer, and more usable.

I care about practical systems: things that can be understood, shipped, maintained, and improved.

## What Usually Does Not Fit

I am usually not the right person for:

- vague “join our idea” messages with no scope
    
- unpaid build requests
    
- generic outsourcing spam
    
- projects without a clear owner, timeline, or decision process
    
- requests that require private credentials, identity documents, or sensitive access before trust is established
    

If the message is clear and serious, I am happy to read it.
