## Reading Notes

_Technical Blogging_ is about the long game of writing on the internet.

The book is useful because it treats a technical blog as a system: topic selection, publishing cadence, audience, search, credibility, community, and maintenance all matter. A blog is not only a place to post finished thoughts; it is a way to compound expertise over time.

## Useful Ideas

### Choose a Clear Center

A strong blog needs a recognizable center of gravity. It can still have range, but readers should understand what kind of thinking they will find there.

For a personal engineering site, that center can be projects, reading notes, architecture decisions, and product experiments.

### Write for Future Discovery

Technical posts often become useful months or years later. Titles, summaries, examples, and internal links should help future readers understand why the post exists and whether it solves their problem.

### Consistency Builds Trust

Publishing does not need to be loud. It needs to be repeatable. A quiet archive of useful notes can become more valuable than a burst of polished announcements.

## Why I Keep It Nearby

This book fits the purpose of Mohetios.dev: a durable engineering notebook, not a social feed. It is a reminder to keep writing useful, discoverable, and connected to real work.
