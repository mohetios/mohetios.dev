## About Me

I’m Mohetios — a product-minded software engineer with around 15 years of practical experience building software across web, mobile, backend systems, dashboards, internal platforms, connected products, and cloud-based workflows.

Most of my work sits between product thinking and engineering execution. I like working where an idea, business process, or technical capability needs to become something usable: a dashboard, a workflow, an internal platform, a mobile app, a financial system, a monitoring interface, or a cloud-connected product.

Over the years, my work has moved through many layers of software: CMS platforms, backend APIs, mobile applications, IoT-style systems, real-time interfaces, platform dashboards, financial workflows, and serverless deployments. That path shaped how I think about engineering.

For me, useful software is not just frontend, backend, or infrastructure. It is a working system that connects people, data, business rules, product flow, and real-world operation.

Mohetios.dev is my technical notebook and personal engineering lab. I use it to write about the things I build, study, and refine: product engineering, frontend architecture, full-stack web development, cloud/serverless systems, AI-assisted workflows, open-source work, connected systems, and long-term experiments around software, science, and invention.

The goal of this site is simple: to document the slow craft of building better systems.

Not only finished projects. Not only polished opinions. But the thinking behind the work — decisions, trade-offs, experiments, failures, notes, and patterns that may become useful later.

## What I Work On

My current focus is around practical product systems:

- full-stack web applications
- dashboards and internal platforms
- React, Next.js, Vue, Nuxt, and TypeScript systems
- API-driven product flows with GraphQL and REST
- mobile applications with React Native and Expo
- Cloudflare, serverless, and edge-oriented workflows
- real-time interfaces, messaging, and local-first behavior
- AI-assisted product workflows
- open-source experiments and technical documentation
- connected-product and IoT-style ideas

I care about software that can be understood, maintained, shipped, and improved. Technical novelty is interesting, but it only matters when it serves a real product, a real workflow, or a real human need.

## How I Think About Engineering

Good engineering starts before code.

It starts with understanding the flow, the constraint, the user, the business rule, and the reason the system needs to exist.

Code is only one part of the work. The harder part is shaping the system so that the pieces fit together: interface, data, API, state, permission, deployment, operation, and future change.

That is the kind of work I enjoy most.

Taking something unclear and making it usable.

Giving structure to complexity.

Turning ideas into systems that can actually work.

## Why Mohetios

Mohetios is the name I use for my public technical identity.

It represents a small but serious direction: building in public, writing honestly, keeping ideas open, and treating software as a craft.

Some notes here are practical. Some are experimental. Some are part of long-term research or unfinished thinking.

The common thread is systems.

How they are designed.  
How they break.  
How they become useful.  
How ideas unfold into working things.
